                Camcorders
              

              
While
                  many consumers use DSLRs and smartphones to record videos, there is still decent market demand for
                  camcorders and action cameras. Camcorders are a kind of video camera that was widely popular in the
                  90s. Current iterations record digitally and utilize digital storage media, such as internal flash
                  memory and SD cards. Action
                  cameras, on the other hand, are a newer type of camera that gives users an incredible amount of
                  mobility. They’re often used by people who love extreme sports.

              
Adorama
                  carries a wide range of action cameras and accessories, as well as consumer and professional

Enjoy
                  Superior Audio & Zoom with Camcorders

              
Camcorders
                  were a big part of what made personal video such a big hit in the 90s. Most people who grew up in the
                  90s probably have footage of themselves recorded via camcorder. These are also the cameras used by
                  many aspiring filmmakers to create their first film projects. 

              
Far
                  from being relics of the past, camcorders have evolved with the times. Since their sensors tend to be
                  smaller than those found in DSLRs and even some smartphones, camcorders generally have greater zoom
                  ranges. Some are even capable of zooming up to 25X—something
                  you won’t be able to do with larger-sensor cameras without the help of expensive telephoto lenses.
                  Some camcorders also accommodate longer-lasting external batteries. Audio is considered to be superior
                  if you own a high-end camcorder, thanks to its XLR input jacks, microphone mounts, and dedicated audio
                  dials. 

              
Capture
                  360-degree Movement with Action Cameras

              
Action
                  cameras are capable of capturing videos and photos in extreme conditions. Most extreme sportsmen enjoy
                  using these cameras because of their portability, making it easier to operate while performing
                  death-defying stunts. While this type of video camera is synonymous with the brand GoPro, there are
                  other brands out there (such as DJI, Freewell,
                  and Fotodiox)
                  that manufacture action cameras that are just as capable.

              
Aside
                  from its portable form-factor, there are other reasons why the action camera is a must-buy for some
                  consumers. The ability to capture ultra-wide (also known as fish-eye) footage is one of those reasons.
                  There is also a seemingly endless number of ways users can mount their cameras: on their bikes, on top
                  of their helmets, by their glider’s wings—the
                  possibilities are endless!
